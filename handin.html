Development

For this development site, I chose three sites that have filtering, aggregator and sorting functionality and compared factors they offer to inform my design for a similar site. I chose three sites that I use frequently, Depop, Pinterest and Google Maps. They all offer different ways to like and save liked posts, and in the process I was surprised to find that such analysis would influence my design decisions.

Part 1: Competitive Analysis

For each site, I compared and analysed different factors and functionality they offer.

Depop

Most successful factor: “Like” Button - Easy to like an item without extra hassle such as a drop down menu. User flow and logic is simple.

Least successful factor: Unsortable List of Favourites - it should be easy for users to sort the list based on items, price etc, for easy view of their list. Without it, the list is massive and hard to use.

Pinterest

Most successful factor: Save to Custom Lists - The ability to save to custom lists when one likes an item is powerful, it allows categorisation by users and allows more freedom when it comes to liking items. For example, users can create a list called “books to read” and “clothes to wear”.

Least successful factor: Auto Select List to Save to - This functionality is annoying, smart-selecting the list user might want to put something in is in theory useful, but the number of times they get it wrong is irritating and leads to the user having to go out of their way into the wrong list, remove the wrong item, then come back and pinning the same item to the correct list again.

Google Maps

Sort List of Favourites - this is very useful as users now have the control over their saved items. They can sort based on distances and prices.

Least successful factor: Add Notes to Favourited Item - I personally found it to be unnecessary as I did not use the functionality even once, although I could see how someone else might want to use it as a reminder to why they saved a certain item. For me it clutters up the list with the “add note” button.

Part 2: Functionality

Based on what worked well and didn’t, I would like to design an interface that contains a list of features that did work well:

  • “Like” button
  • “Like” button reacts when hovering
  • List of Favourites
  • Edit List of Favourites
  • Sort List of Favourites

The idea I came up with is inspired by my experience as a student at RISD. I constantly get asked questions such as “do you know a voice actor for $50”, “do you know someone who can remove an arm in VFX”, “do you know a Graphic designer for a concert campaign”. The problem is that as someone with a gig, people usually turn to instagram instead of Fiver or Craiglists, because Instagram has a network effect and one is more likely to find someone with mutual connections and are local. I decided to make a site that isn’t filled with gig posts but rather works of real students. The interface is similar to Pinterest, except for most Pinterest work, it is impossible to trace back to the original artist for their work (pins are saved many many times), while for FOAM, one can save a list called “Ceramics” and be able to contact the real artist. In short, FOAM is a Pinterest-like portfolio site for art school students, a local-artist discovery site for clients to keep track of their favourite artists. In this way, clients don’t have to go through tons of applications and instead always have the ability to message artists they like, without having to go through Instagram!

Foam Artist Finder: Pinterest for Local Artists

Filtering categories: Major, Location, Availability

Sorting feature: Price, Artist Name

Aggregator section: Favorites gallery

Item cards: Photograph image, title, major, name, location, availability status

I will be using filter to filter by Major, Location and whether a user is Open to commission. Similarly, I will be using a sorting feature to sort the users by Price. Lastly, aggregation will be used to add favorites to a gallery (there will be custom lists as well)
